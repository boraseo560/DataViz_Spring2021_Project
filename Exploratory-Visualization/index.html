How bad is the air quality in South Korea?

* What is PM? and why it is bad?

Particulate Matter (PM) is a complex mixture of solid particles and liquid droplets. By the diameters of the particles, PM is grouped into two categories: PM 10 (2.5-10 μm) and PM 2.5 (less than 2.5 μm) (Credit: EPA) Because of the size, it is easy to inhale the pollutants without filtering. Also, it can be harmful to the human respiratory system.

* Minimum guideline level of PM 10 and PM 2.5 of South Korea, USA and WHO (Annual)

South Korea: less than 15㎍/㎥ (PM 2.5), less than 50㎍/㎥ (PM 10)

USA: less than 12㎍/㎥ (PM 2.5), less than 50㎍/㎥ (PM 10)

WHO: less than 10㎍/㎥ (PM 2.5), less than 20㎍/㎥ (PM 10)
